I.)Important Definitions

-A modem is a hardware device that allows a computer to send and receive data over a cable or satellite connection
-A modem router incorporate a modem and a router in a single apparel or in a seperate way.
-A modem router is the main componenet in a small computer network.
-The modem router is wired to the calbe company's coaxial cable or the telephone company's DSL phone line.
-In the case of this presentation, we are going to use the Comcast/Xfinity modem.

II.)Caution and Warning

-To avoid potential shock, always unplug the power cord from the wall outlet or other power source before disconnecting it from the modem rear panel
-Do not install the modem on top of any other electrical equipment or install any other equipment on top of this device.
-Never install any modem cable during a lightning storm.
-Never install modem in wet locations unless the jack is specifically designed for wet locations.
-Wipe the cable modem with a clean, dry cloth. Never use cleaning fluid or similar chemicals. Do not spray cleaners directly on the unit or use forced air to remove dust.

B.Steps of Installion

Step 1: Plug in & power up

-Place your modem near a cable wall outlet, in a central location, away from windows and external walls.
-Screw one end of the coax cable to the Cable Inport on your modem. Screw the other end to the cable wall outlet or one of the ports on the splitter.
-Plug the power cord in the back of your modem. Plug the other end into an electrical outlet.
-Wait until the Power, US/DS, and Online lights remain solid for 1 minute. The lights may ash for up to 20 minutes, and your modem may reboot.

Step 2: Connect to the Internet Connect with Wi-Fi

-Wait for the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz Wi-Fi lights to START lighting.
-Find the default Network Name (SSID) and Password(Security Key) on the sticker on the side, back or bottom of your modem.
-Access the Wi-Fi settings on your personal device. Then select the Network Name (SSID) printed on the sticker (referenced above) from the list of availble networks.
-When prompted, enter the password printed on the sticker to connect to the network.

Step 3: How to Connect with Ethernet Cable

-Plug the ethernet cable into one of the open ethernet ports on your modem until it clicks. Plug the other end into the ethernet port in your computer.
-If this worked, the LEDs next to the ethernet ports on your modem will light up.

Step 4: Activate your service

-Once connected to the network, if you're not automatically presented with a welcome message to begin setup, open a web browser and go to www.xfinity.com
-Follow the on-screen prompts to verify your account. You may also be prompted to give your Wi-Fi network a new name and password that you will easily remember.
-Wait while your Wireless Gateway completes activation. It may reboot during this process. Activation should be complete when the Power, US/DS, and online lights remain solid for 1 minute and the 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz lights start binking.
If you were promted to give your Wi-Fi network a new name and pass, be sure to connect your wireless devices using this new information.

V.) Troubleshooting of Internet Connection Problem After Installing

-Unplug the modem, then plug it back in
-Unplug the modem's power cord
-Count to 15, which lets the modem power down and plug it back in
-Wait two minutes for the modem to initialize the connection
-Check for a temporary service interruption
There may be an outage in your area. Wait 15 to 30 minutes and try to get on the Internet again.
In some cases, service may be interrupted due to non-payment. If you think this may have happened, again, contact your internet provider, and follow the prompts to hear or pay your account balance.
-Restart all internet connections
-Be sure to turn OFF your equipment in a specific order, then turn them back ON in the order below.
-If you are installing a modem that is purchased from somewhere other than Xfinity, refer to our Xfinity certified cable modem page to make sure your modem is compatible with our network.
-When your computer connects to the internet, it is assigned to an internet protocol address (IP address), usually a different address each time you connect. If you're experiencing slow or no signal, the problem may be resolved by refreshing your computer's IP address.
-Refresh your computer's Internet signal (IP Address)

VI.)Frequently Asked Questions

How do I log out of my Wireless account so I can connect with another machine?

Type "Logout" in the address bar (without the quotes), and the session will terminate. (This action may vary depend on the device)

How many computers can I connect to the wireless service?

It depends on your wireless strenght or gigabyte (internet package) you subscribed from the company. Your internet package matter on your Wi-Fi signal strength. In other words, when you pay more you will get a stronger signal and be able to connect up to 10 devices.
